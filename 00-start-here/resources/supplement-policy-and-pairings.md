# Supplement Policy And Pairings

This curriculum is `free-first`. Commercial supplements are `precision tools`, not the foundation. This file makes that rule explicit so buying a book is a deliberate decision, not a reflex.

See [books and supplies](./books-and-supplies.md) for the broader materials list.

## The Rule

Buy a supplement only when all of the following are true:

- you have made a real first pass through the free casebook and the primary law
- the subject is still genuinely sticky after that pass
- you can name the specific thing you do not understand
- one targeted book would plausibly fix it

If you cannot name the specific gap, you are not ready to buy. Read the primary material again.

## The Supplement Families

- `Examples & Explanations (E&E)` — problem-driven; best when you understand the rules but cannot apply them
- `Short & Happy Guides` — fast orientation; best at the very start of a sticky subject
- `Understanding` series — denser doctrinal treatment; best for a structured second pass
- `Concepts and Insights` — conceptual framing; best when the subject's logic, not its rules, is the problem
- `Glannon Guide` — civil-procedure-strong, multiple-choice-driven practice

## Match The Family To The Gap

- confused about `how to apply a rule you already know` -> `E&E`
- confused about `the basic map of the subject` -> `Short & Happy`
- confused about `how the doctrine fits together` -> `Understanding` or `Concepts and Insights`
- need `timed practice questions` -> `Glannon Guide` or `E&E` problem sets

## Recommended Pairings By Subject

- `civil procedure` -> `Glannon Guide` or `E&E Civil Procedure`
- `torts` -> `E&E Torts` or `Short & Happy Guide to Torts`
- `contracts` -> `E&E Contracts` or `Short & Happy Guide to Contracts`
- `property` -> `E&E Property`
- `criminal law` -> `E&E Criminal Law`
- `constitutional law` -> a concise con-law supplement, used only after the primary text
- `evidence` -> a concise hearsay-focused supplement (`Understanding Evidence` or an `E&E`)
- `business associations` -> a corporate-law or BA supplement once entity vocabulary slows you
- `commercial law / UCC` -> a secured-transactions supplement, only after reading the code
- `administrative law` -> a supplement only if admin becomes a career subject

## Free-First Substitutes

Before buying, the free stack already covers most needs:

- `Wex` (Cornell LII) for quick doctrinal definitions
- `CALI` lessons and `eLangdell` open casebooks for guided practice
- the primary `rule`, `statute`, or `restatement` text the casebook excerpts

These are listed in [open resource library](./open-resource-library.md).

## Bottom Line

One supplement per sticky subject, chosen for a named gap, after the free pass. Never a full shelf bought in advance.
