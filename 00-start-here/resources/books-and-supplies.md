# Books And Supplies

This list is intentionally selective and `free-first`.

If you are self-studying, you should start with open casebooks, official rules, and public legal research tools. Do not buy a full stack of expensive casebooks unless you later decide you want them for depth or collection purposes.

## Default Strategy

1. Use `H2O`, `Harvard/Yale/Columbia/Penn/Duke public guides`, `GovInfo`, `Oyez`, and `CourtListener` first.
2. Build your own outline from the free materials.
3. Buy a supplement only if a subject still feels muddy after real effort.

## Free-First Core Stack

These should cover a surprising amount of the curriculum without spending much:

- `H2O Open Casebooks`
- `Harvard Legal Research Strategy`
- `Yale Law Library Research Guides`
- `Columbia Legal Writing Resources`
- `Penn The Regulatory Review`
- `Duke UCC Research Guide`
- `GovInfo`
- `Oyez`
- `CourtListener`
- `Indigo Book`

## Optional Paid Buys

### 1. Getting to Maybe: How to Excel on Law School Exams, Second Edition

- Why: still one of the best books on issue-spotting and law-school exam method
- When to buy: only if you want a paid exam-skills companion after the free doctrinal pass
- Publisher page: <https://cap-press.com/books/isbn/9781594607349/Getting-to-Maybe-Second-Edition>

### 2. A Short and Happy Guide to Acing Your 1L Year

- Why: compact orientation to the 1L doctrinal map and study habits
- When to buy: optional
- Publisher page: <https://www.westacademic.com/Gershowitzs-A-Short-Happy-Guide-to-Acing-Your-1L-Year-9798892098748>

### 3. Black's Law Dictionary

- Why: useful, but not mandatory on day one
- Free substitute: use your running glossary, your casebook vocabulary lists, and the underlying rule or statute first

## Subject-Specific Paid Fallbacks

### Civil Procedure

- `The Glannon Guide to Civil Procedure`
- `Examples & Explanations for Civil Procedure`

### Torts

- `A Short & Happy Guide to Torts`
- Free-first rule: lean on Harvard H2O and the official cases first

### Contracts

- `A Short & Happy Guide to Contracts`
- Publisher page: <https://www.westacademic.com/A-Short-and-Happy-Guide-to-Contracts-6>
- `Examples & Explanations for Contracts`
- Publisher page: <https://aspenpublishing.com/products/ee-blum-contracts9>

### Property

- `Examples & Explanations for Property`
- Buy the latest edition available from Aspen or a used recent edition if cost matters

### Criminal Law

- `Examples & Explanations for Criminal Law`
- Publisher page: <https://aspenpublishing.com/products/baughman-ee-criminallaw9>

### Evidence

- A concise evidence supplement from the `Short & Happy`, `Understanding`, or `Examples & Explanations` line is enough

### Administrative Law / Business Associations / Secured Transactions

- Buy only if those subjects become sticky after using the open materials first

## Physical Supplies

- one binder or digital notebook per year or major section
- one running glossary file
- one outline file per subject
- one attack-outline file per subject
- flashcards only for rules that truly require memorization

## What Not To Buy Up Front

- full commercial bar prep
- every commercial outline series
- expensive hardcopy casebooks when the open version is sufficient
- multiple supplements for the same subject before trying one

## Bottom Line

Treat paid books as `precision tools`, not the foundation. The foundation of this folder is free.
