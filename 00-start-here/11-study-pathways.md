# Study Pathways (By Goal, Not By Year)

The `1L -> 2L -> 3L` order is the default, but it is not the only way through. If you would rather study by destination than by calendar, pick one pathway below and follow its subject order. Each subject still uses its own guide and second source; a pathway only changes the sequence.

Most subjects can be started without finishing the year before them. A subject guide names any real prerequisite at the top, under `Why It Belongs`.

## How To Use A Pathway

- pick the one pathway that matches where you are headed
- follow its subject order, using each subject's guide and its alternate casebook
- keep the same deliverables: subject outline, attack outline, rule sheet, timed `IRAC`
- mark each subject done with the [subject progress checklist](../templates/subject-progress-checklist-template.md)

For clerkship, appellate, public-law, or academic interest. Structure first, then rights, then the administrative state.

## If You Just Want One Subject

You do not need a pathway at all. Most subjects can be started cold. Open the subject guide and begin; it names any prerequisite at the top. The dependencies that actually matter are few: constitutional rights builds on constitutional structure; criminal procedure builds on criminal law and constitutional structure; business associations and the UCC build on contracts; administrative law builds on legislation and constitutional structure; conflict of laws builds on civil procedure.
