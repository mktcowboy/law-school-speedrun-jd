# Landmark Case Canon

A short, high-yield list of the cases that anchor each `1L` and core `2L` subject. These are the opinions `T10/Ivy` casebooks build around and that exams assume you know cold.

This is a `spine`, not a complete reading list. Read the full opinions through [CourtListener](https://www.courtlistener.com/) or [Oyez](https://www.oyez.org/) when an excerpt feels too compressed. Use it to check coverage, not to replace the casebooks.

## How To Use

- when you finish a subject, confirm you can state the `rule` each case stands for in one sentence
- if you cannot, that case is a gap — reread it
- pair this with your `rule sheet` and attack outline

## Civil Procedure

- `Pennoyer v. Neff` — territorial roots of personal jurisdiction
- `International Shoe v. Washington` — minimum-contacts test for personal jurisdiction
- `World-Wide Volkswagen v. Woodson` — purposeful availment and foreseeability
- `Goodyear v. Brown` and `Daimler AG v. Bauman` — general jurisdiction reaches only where a defendant is "at home"
- `Ford Motor Co. v. Montana Eighth Judicial District` — relatedness in modern specific jurisdiction
- `Louisville & Nashville R.R. v. Mottley` — the well-pleaded-complaint rule for federal-question jurisdiction
- `Erie R. Co. v. Tompkins` — federal courts apply state substantive law in diversity
- `Hanna v. Plumer` — Erie analysis when a Federal Rule is on point
- `Mullane v. Central Hanover Bank` — the due-process floor for notice
- `Bell Atlantic v. Twombly` and `Ashcroft v. Iqbal` — plausibility pleading standard
- `Hickman v. Taylor` — work-product protection in discovery
- `Celotex v. Catrett` — the summary-judgment burden

## Torts

- `Vosburg v. Putney` — intent for battery; take-your-victim principle
- `Garratt v. Dailey` — intent satisfied by substantial certainty
- `Vincent v. Lake Erie Transp. Co.` — private necessity as an incomplete privilege
- `United States v. Carroll Towing` — the Hand formula for breach
- `Byrne v. Boadle` — res ipsa loquitur lets breach be inferred
- `Palsgraf v. Long Island R.R.` — duty and the foreseeable-plaintiff limit on proximate cause
- `Overseas Tankship v. Morts Dock (The Wagon Mound No. 1)` — proximate cause limited to foreseeable harm
- `Summers v. Tice` — alternative liability shifts the causation burden among negligent defendants
- `MacPherson v. Buick` — abolition of the privity requirement in negligence
- `Escola v. Coca-Cola` (Traynor concurrence) — seedbed of strict products liability
- `Tarasoff v. Regents` — affirmative duty to warn

## Contracts

- `Hawkins v. McGee` — expectation damages ("the hairy hand")
- `Hamer v. Sidway` — consideration as bargained-for forbearance
- `Lucy v. Zehmer` — objective theory of assent
- `Lefkowitz v. Great Minneapolis Surplus Store` — when an advertisement is an offer
- `Wood v. Lucy, Lady Duff-Gordon` — implied promise and the duty of good faith (Cardozo)
- `Raffles v. Wichelhaus` (the Peerless case) — mutual misunderstanding defeats assent
- `Hadley v. Baxendale` — foreseeability limit on consequential damages
- `Hoffman v. Red Owl Stores` — promissory estoppel in negotiations
- `Jacob & Youngs v. Kent` — substantial performance and the measure of damages
- `Peevyhouse v. Garland Coal` — expectation damages: cost of performance versus diminution in value
- `Williams v. Walker-Thomas Furniture` — procedural and substantive unconscionability

## Property

- `Pierson v. Post` — first possession and capture
- `Johnson v. M'Intosh` — chain of title and the discovery doctrine
- `Jacque v. Steenberg Homes` — the right to exclude
- `Javins v. First National Realty` — implied warranty of habitability in residential leases
- `Boomer v. Atlantic Cement Co.` — nuisance remedy and permanent damages over injunction
- `Shelley v. Kraemer` — state action and racially restrictive covenants
- `Pennsylvania Coal v. Mahon` — regulatory takings
- `Penn Central Transp. Co. v. New York City` — the regulatory-takings balancing test
- `Lucas v. South Carolina Coastal Council` — total economic wipeout as a per se taking
- `Kelo v. City of New London` — public use under the Takings Clause

## Criminal Law

- `Martin v. State` — the voluntary-act (actus reus) requirement
- `Morissette v. United States` — the presumption of a mens rea requirement; rejecting silent strict liability
- `Regina v. Cunningham` — recklessness and the meaning of "malice"
- `People v. Conley` — intent and the mental-state hierarchy
- `Commonwealth v. Carroll` and `People v. Anderson` — the split on what premeditation requires
- `People v. Goetz` — the objective standard for self-defense reasonableness
- `Pinkerton v. United States` — co-conspirator liability for foreseeable crimes
- `Regina v. Dudley and Stephens` — necessity is no defense to murder
- `M'Naghten's Case` — the insanity standard

## Constitutional Law I — Structure

- `Marbury v. Madison` — judicial review
- `McCulloch v. Maryland` — implied powers and the Necessary and Proper Clause
- `Gibbons v. Ogden` — scope of the Commerce Clause
- `Wickard v. Filburn` — aggregation and the reach of the New Deal commerce power
- `United States v. Lopez` — the modern outer limit on the Commerce Clause
- `NFIB v. Sebelius` — Commerce Clause limit and the independent taxing power
- `Youngstown Sheet & Tube v. Sawyer` (Jackson concurrence) — separation of powers
- `INS v. Chadha` — bicameralism, presentment, and the legislative veto
- `New York v. United States` and `Printz v. United States` — the anti-commandeering principle

## Constitutional Law II — Rights And Liberties

- `United States v. Carolene Products`, footnote 4 — origins of tiered scrutiny
- `Lochner v. New York` and `West Coast Hotel v. Parrish` — rise and fall of economic due process
- `Brown v. Board of Education` — equal protection and segregation
- `Loving v. Virginia` — strict scrutiny for race classifications
- `Washington v. Davis` — discriminatory intent is required for an equal-protection claim
- `Craig v. Boren` — intermediate scrutiny for sex classifications
- `Griswold v. Connecticut` — substantive due process and the privacy line
- `Roe v. Wade` and `Planned Parenthood v. Casey` — the abortion-right line (historical baseline)
- `Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization` (2022) — overruled Roe and Casey; no constitutional abortion right
- `Lawrence v. Texas` — liberty and intimate conduct
- `Obergefell v. Hodges` — same-sex marriage as a fundamental right

### First Amendment — Speech

- `Tinker v. Des Moines` — student political speech
- `Brandenburg v. Ohio` — incitement and the imminent-lawless-action test
- `New York Times v. Sullivan` — defamation and the actual-malice standard
- `Miller v. California` — the obscenity test
- `Central Hudson Gas & Elec. v. Public Service Commission` — commercial-speech scrutiny

### First Amendment — Religion

- `Employment Division v. Smith` — neutral, generally applicable laws and free exercise
- `Lemon v. Kurtzman` — the (now-displaced) Establishment Clause test
- `Kennedy v. Bremerton School District` (2022) — Establishment analysis by history and tradition

## Legislation And Statutory Interpretation

These anchor the `legislation and regulation` guide. They show the live fight between text, purpose, and the canons.

- `Holy Trinity Church v. United States` — purposivism: spirit over letter
- `TVA v. Hill` — plain-meaning textualism even at high cost (the snail darter)
- `King v. Burwell` — statutory context and scheme over an isolated phrase
- `Bostock v. Clayton County` (2020) — textualism applied to Title VII
- `West Virginia v. EPA` (2022) — the major-questions doctrine as a clear-statement rule

## Evidence

- `Old Chief v. United States` — Rule 403 balancing and stipulations
- `Tome v. United States` — prior consistent statements
- `Huddleston v. United States` — 404(b) other-acts evidence
- `Daubert v. Merrell Dow` — admissibility of expert testimony
- `Crawford v. Washington` — Confrontation Clause and testimonial hearsay

## Criminal Procedure

- `Katz v. United States` — the reasonable-expectation-of-privacy test
- `Terry v. Ohio` — stop and frisk on reasonable suspicion
- `Mapp v. Ohio` — exclusionary rule applied to the states
- `Riley v. California` — warrant requirement for cell-phone searches incident to arrest
- `Miranda v. Arizona` — warnings for custodial interrogation
- `Gideon v. Wainwright` — right to appointed counsel
- `Brady v. Maryland` — the prosecution's duty to disclose exculpatory evidence
- `Strickland v. Washington` — the ineffective-assistance-of-counsel standard
- `Carpenter v. United States` — the digital-era Fourth Amendment

## Business Associations

- `Meinhard v. Salmon` — fiduciary duty among partners
- `Walkovszky v. Carlton` — piercing the corporate veil
- `Dodge v. Ford Motor Co.` — shareholder-primacy framing
- `Smith v. Van Gorkom` — the duty of care and informed decisions
- `Aronson v. Lewis` — demand futility in derivative litigation
- `Weinberger v. UOP` — entire-fairness review of conflicted transactions
- `Unocal v. Mesa Petroleum` and `Revlon v. MacAndrews & Forbes` — board duties in takeovers
- `In re Caremark` — board oversight liability

## Administrative Law

- `Chevron v. NRDC` — deference to agency statutory interpretation (now overruled; know it as background)
- `Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo` (2024) — overruled Chevron; courts now interpret statutes de novo
- `Skidmore v. Swift` — persuasive-weight deference
- `Kisor v. Wilkie` — narrowed deference to an agency reading its own regulation (Auer)
- `Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Assn. v. State Farm` — arbitrary-and-capricious (hard-look) review
- `West Virginia v. EPA` (2022) — the major-questions doctrine limits agency authority over major policy
- `Vermont Yankee v. NRDC` — courts cannot impose procedures beyond the APA

## Professional Responsibility

Professional responsibility is rule-driven rather than case-driven. Anchor on the `ABA Model Rules` themselves — especially `1.1`, `1.6`, `1.7`, `1.9`, `3.3`, and `4.1` — rather than a case canon. See [02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/professional-responsibility/README.md](../../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/professional-responsibility/README.md).

## Family Law

A `legacy MEE` subject, mostly state law over a constitutional floor. The rights cases overlap Constitutional Law II. See [02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/family-law/README.md](../../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/family-law/README.md).

- `Loving v. Virginia` — the right to marry
- `Obergefell v. Hodges` — same-sex marriage
- `Stanley v. Illinois` — unwed-father parental rights and due process
- `Troxel v. Granville` — parental authority over third-party visitation
- `Michael H. v. Gerald D.` — competing presumptions of paternity

## Trusts And Estates

A `legacy MEE` subject that is code-anchored rather than case-driven. Anchor on the `Uniform Probate Code` and `Uniform Trust Code`, with your state's variations, rather than a case canon — and on the future-interests and `Rule Against Perpetuities` material shared with property. See [02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/trusts-and-estates/README.md](../../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/trusts-and-estates/README.md).

## Conflict Of Laws

A `legacy MEE` subject that also runs underneath civil procedure and Full Faith and Credit. See [02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/conflict-of-laws/README.md](../../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/conflict-of-laws/README.md).

- `Erie R. Co. v. Tompkins` — state substantive law in federal diversity cases
- `Klaxon Co. v. Stentor Electric` — a federal court applies the forum state's choice-of-law rules
- `Babcock v. Jackson` — the turn to interest analysis and the most-significant-relationship approach
- `Allstate Insurance Co. v. Hague` — the constitutional floor for applying forum law

## A Note On The Canon

These cases are the floor, not the ceiling. They give you shared vocabulary with any `T10/Ivy` course and any bar outline. Once you can state each holding cold, your reading time is better spent on the cases your specific casebook emphasizes.
