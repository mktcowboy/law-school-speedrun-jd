# Landmark Case Canon

A short, high-yield list of the cases that anchor each `1L` and core `2L` subject. These are the opinions `T10/Ivy` casebooks build around and that exams assume you know cold.

This is a `spine`, not a complete reading list. Case links point to [CourtListener](https://www.courtlistener.com/) opinion searches as a free-law jump point; use [Oyez](https://www.oyez.org/) for Supreme Court summaries, audio, and argument materials when helpful. Use this canon to check coverage, not to replace the casebooks.

## How To Use

- when you finish a subject, confirm you can state the `rule` each case stands for in one sentence
- if you cannot, that case is a gap — reread it
- pair this with your `rule sheet` and attack outline

## Conflict Of Laws

A `legacy MEE` subject that also runs underneath civil procedure and Full Faith and Credit. See [02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/conflict-of-laws/README.md](../../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/conflict-of-laws/README.md).

- [`Erie R. Co. v. Tompkins`](https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=%22Erie+R.+Co.+v.+Tompkins%22&type=o&order_by=score+desc) — state substantive law in federal diversity cases
- [`Klaxon Co. v. Stentor Electric`](https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=%22Klaxon+Co.+v.+Stentor+Electric%22&type=o&order_by=score+desc) — a federal court applies the forum state's choice-of-law rules
- [`Babcock v. Jackson`](https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=%22Babcock+v.+Jackson%22&type=o&order_by=score+desc) — the turn to interest analysis and the most-significant-relationship approach
- [`Allstate Insurance Co. v. Hague`](https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=%22Allstate+Insurance+Co.+v.+Hague%22&type=o&order_by=score+desc) — the constitutional floor for applying forum law

## A Note On The Canon

These cases are the floor, not the ceiling. They give you shared vocabulary with any `T10/Ivy` course and any bar outline. Once you can state each holding cold, your reading time is better spent on the cases your specific casebook emphasizes.
