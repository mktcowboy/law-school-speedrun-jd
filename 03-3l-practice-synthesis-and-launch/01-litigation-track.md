# Litigation Track

Back to the [subject index](../SUBJECTS.md#3l-practice-and-launch).

One of two `3L` practice directions. Pick the track that matches the work you want; you can read both, but build deliverables in one. Pair this with the practice systems in this section.

## Who This Is For

Readers aiming at trial and appellate practice — civil litigation, criminal prosecution or defense, government enforcement, and public-interest litigation.

## Core Subjects To Revisit

Reread these with a litigator's eye, not a student's:

- [civil-procedure](../01-1l-foundation-year/civil-procedure/README.md) — jurisdiction, pleading, discovery, dispositive motions, preclusion
- [evidence](../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/evidence/README.md) — relevance, hearsay, impeachment, the FRE in trial sequence
- [criminal-procedure](../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/criminal-procedure/README.md) — if you lean criminal
- [conflict-of-laws](../02-2l-doctrinal-depth-and-codes/conflict-of-laws/README.md) — when disputes cross state lines
- remedies and federal courts as expansion subjects — justiciability, abstention, injunctions, damages

## Drafting Exercises

- a complaint and an answer on one fact pattern
- a `Rule 12(b)(6)` motion and an opposition
- a summary-judgment brief with a statement of undisputed facts
- a discovery plan: initial disclosures, one interrogatory set, one document request
- a motion in limine on an evidence problem
- one appellate-brief section: statement of the case plus one argument

## Research Emphasis

- binding versus persuasive authority, and validating citations with free tools
- docket research — how real cases are actually litigated
- court-rules discipline
- the record: briefs, transcripts, and oral arguments

## Simulations

- run one mock matter end to end: intake → pleadings → one motion → a mock hearing script
- build the trial notebook and motion outline from [trial-advocacy-and-motion-practice](./trial-advocacy-and-motion-practice/README.md)
- reuse a single record or hypo across motions and trial stages
- use [clinics-externships-and-practice-systems](./clinics-externships-and-practice-systems/README.md) to structure the mock file

## Hiring Relevance

- signals employers read: journal, moot court, a writing sample that is a real brief, clerkship interest, clinic work
- a litigation writing sample should show issue-framing and record citation, not just doctrine
- clerkships reward procedure plus writing; prosecutor and public-defender offices reward trial reps and criminal procedure

## Start Here

Teaching from the `T10/Ivy` band; dockets and opinions from the best free host.

## Deliverables

- one pleading set: complaint, answer, and one motion
- one litigation writing sample: a brief section with record citations
- one mock-matter timeline and trial notebook
