# 3L Practice, Synthesis, And Launch

`3L` is where the curriculum should stop pretending doctrine alone is enough.

This stage is about integrating what you know with actual practice systems, professional judgment, writing, and bar/career launch planning.

It should still be possible to do most of this with free materials plus your own outlines and simulated files.

## Typical 3L Priorities

- clinics and externships
- trial advocacy and drafting
- advanced seminars or capstones
- bar-aligned synthesis
- job transition and practice readiness

## What 3L Should Produce

- cleaner writing
- stronger judgment about process and strategy
- better issue-spotting across subjects
- a more realistic understanding of how lawyers actually work

## Do Not Waste 3L

The self-study version of `3L` should not be a pile of random electives.

Use it to:

- sharpen writing
- simulate practice
- do capstone synthesis
- prepare for the format of the exam or job transition ahead

## Choose A Practice Direction

By `3L` the work splits into two broad directions. Pick the one that matches the job you want and build your deliverables there — read the other for awareness.

- [01-litigation-track.md](./01-litigation-track.md) — trial and appellate practice: procedure, evidence, motions, briefs, and trial simulation
- [02-transactional-track.md](./02-transactional-track.md) — deal and advisory practice: entities, the UCC, drafting, and document sets

Each track names the subjects to revisit, the drafting exercises and simulations to run, the research emphasis, and how the work reads to employers.
