# Legal Research And Writing

## Why It Belongs In 1L

This is not an elective skill. It is the operating system for every doctrinal course.

## Master These First

- finding controlling authority
- distinguishing cases from statutes and rules
- basic citation
- rule synthesis
- memo-style explanation
- short issue-spotting answers

## Start Here

- Harvard Legal Research Strategy: <https://guides.library.harvard.edu/law/researchstrategy>
- Yale Law Library research guides: <https://library.law.yale.edu/research/research-guides>
- GovInfo: <https://www.govinfo.gov/>
- CourtListener: <https://free.law/projects/courtlistener/>
- Columbia legal writing resources: <https://www.law.columbia.edu/academics/experiential/legal-writing/writing-center/legal-writing-resources>
- Indigo Book: <https://indigobook.github.io/>

## Core Practice

- brief one case
- brief one statute or rule
- write one short memo
- cite your sources cleanly

## Best Supplement If You Need One

- `The Bluebook` only if you need formal citation depth

## Deliverables

- one research map
- one citation cheat sheet
- one `500 to 800 word` memo
