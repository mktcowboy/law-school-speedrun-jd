# 1L Reading Sequence And Free Sources

This guide is the reading-heavy roadmap for the `1L` portion of the curriculum.

The goal is to read a serious amount of excellent material in the traditional `1L` subjects without defaulting to expensive commercial books.

The working rule is simple:

1. start with relevant free public material from a current `top-10` law school
2. read the actual rule text, statute, or constitutional provision
3. use full opinions, audio, or explainers only after you have read the assigned material

## Default 1L Order

1. Civil Procedure
2. Torts
3. Contracts
4. Property
5. Criminal Law
6. Constitutional Law
7. Legal Research and Writing throughout

## Civil Procedure

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard H2O Civil Procedure 2024: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/9188-civil-procedure-2024/>
2. Official Federal Rules of Civil Procedure: <https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/current-rules-practice-procedure/federal-rules-civil-procedure>
3. CourtListener for any full opinion you want to see unedited: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>
4. Yale Docket Research if you want to understand how cases live in the court system beyond the casebook: <https://library.law.yale.edu/research-guides/docket-research>
5. Stanford Docket Research if you want another guide on docket coverage, retrieval, and limits: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/dockets>
6. Duke Court Rules if you want a stronger map of how procedural rules fit together: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/court-rules>

### First-Pass Priorities

- subject-matter jurisdiction
- personal jurisdiction
- venue
- pleading
- motions to dismiss
- discovery
- summary judgment

### Read Slowly

- `International Shoe`
- `Erie`
- `Twombly`
- `Iqbal`
- `FRCP 8`, `12`, `26`, and `56`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain how a civil case enters court, why a court may or may not hear it, what the pleadings must do, and how discovery and dispositive motions shape the case.

## Torts

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard H2O Torts!: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/2566-torts/>
2. CourtListener for full opinions when you want more context: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- intentional torts
- negligence
- duty
- breach
- actual cause
- proximate cause
- damages
- defenses

### Read Slowly

- `Vosburg v. Putney`
- `Garratt v. Dailey`
- `Palsgraf`
- `United States v. Carroll Towing Co.`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can walk through a negligence problem in order and explain why duty, breach, causation, and damages are analyzed separately.

## Contracts

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard H2O Contracts: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/11568-contracts/>
2. Re-read the Restatement and statutory excerpts embedded in the casebook
3. Save Uniform Commercial Code work for your later `2L` commercial-law pass, except for very basic sale-of-goods awareness

### First-Pass Priorities

- offer and acceptance
- objective assent
- consideration
- promissory estoppel
- interpretation
- conditions
- breach
- expectation, reliance, and restitution

### Read Slowly

- the `consideration` chapters
- the `promissory estoppel` chapters
- the `remedies` chapters

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain when a promise becomes legally enforceable and what kind of interest a contract remedy is trying to protect.

## Property

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard H2O Open Source Property: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/510-open-source-property/>
2. CourtListener for any case you want to read in full: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- possession and acquisition
- the right to exclude
- estates and future interests at a working level
- landlord-tenant basics
- servitudes at a high level

### Read Slowly

- `Pierson v. Post`
- `Johnson v. M'Intosh`
- `Jacque v. Steenberg Homes`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain the basic architecture of ownership, possession, estates, and the major private-law limits on land use.

## Criminal Law

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard H2O Criminal Law Spring 2021: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/2459-criminal-law-spring-2021/>
2. When a case turns on statutory wording, pull the underlying statute and read it in full
3. CourtListener for full opinions when an excerpt feels too compressed: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- actus reus
- mens rea
- causation
- homicide
- attempt
- complicity
- justification and excuse defenses

### Read Slowly

- `Martin v. State`
- `Regina v. Cunningham`
- `People v. Conley`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain why criminal liability normally requires both a prohibited act and a culpable mental state.

## Constitutional Law

### Read In This Order

1. National Archives Constitution transcript: <https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution>
2. Yale Avalon constitutional document record: <https://avalon.law.yale.edu/subject_menus/constpap.asp>
3. Congress.gov Constitution Annotated: <https://constitution.congress.gov/>
4. Harvard H2O U.S. Constitution materials: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/1132/>
5. Columbia Global Freedom of Expression for speech-focused comparative reading: <https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/>
6. Oyez for case background and oral argument context: <https://www.oyez.org/>
7. Stanford Supreme Court Materials if you want a better map of opinions, briefs, transcripts, and records: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/supremecourt>
8. Yale Constitution Day Resources for a deeper constitutional-research layer: <https://library.law.yale.edu/research-guides/constitution-day-resources>
9. Columbia Teaching Freedom of Expression portal for structured speech-law expansion: <https://teaching.globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- judicial review as background structure
- separation of powers and federalism at a high level
- state action
- First Amendment basics
- equal protection
- due process
- levels of scrutiny

### Read Slowly

- `Marbury v. Madison`
- `Brown v. Board of Education`
- one speech case
- one due-process case
- one equal-protection case

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can identify the constitutional framework, the doctrinal test, and the institutional actor before jumping into policy argument.

## Legal Research And Writing

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard Legal Research Strategy: <https://guides.library.harvard.edu/law/researchstrategy>
2. Harvard statutes guide: <https://guides.library.harvard.edu/law/statutes>
3. Yale Law Library research guides: <https://library.law.yale.edu/research/research-guides>
4. Stanford Free and Low-Cost Legal Research: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/free-lowcost>
5. Stanford Case Finding and Advanced Searching Strategies: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/cases>
6. Yale SAW research guide: <https://library.law.yale.edu/research-guides/guide-substantial-and-supervised-analytical-writing-saw-research>
7. Penn Legal Research Fundamentals: <https://law.upenn.libguides.com/researchfundamentals>
8. Columbia legal writing resources: <https://www.law.columbia.edu/academics/experiential/legal-writing/writing-center/legal-writing-resources>
9. Columbia memo writing checklist: <https://www.law.columbia.edu/sites/default/files/2022-08/WC%20Memo%20Checklist.pdf>
10. Stanford Writing Notes and Comments for Journal Members if you want a bridge into longer scholarly writing: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/writingnotes>
11. NYU Writing a Student Note for upper-level writing development: <https://www.law.nyu.edu/students/studentwriting>
12. Indigo Book: <https://indigobook.github.io/>
13. GovInfo: <https://www.govinfo.gov/>
14. CourtListener: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- primary versus secondary authority
- case finding
- statute and rule locating
- reading a case for holding versus dicta
- memo structure
- rule synthesis
- basic citation discipline

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can find controlling authority, explain the rule in clean prose, and support that explanation with usable citations.

## General 1L Rule

If you have to choose between:

- reading another summary of the doctrine
- and reading the actual casebook section or operative rule text

read the casebook section or rule text first.
