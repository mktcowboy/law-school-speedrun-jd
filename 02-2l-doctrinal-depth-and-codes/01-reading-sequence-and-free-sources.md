# 2L Reading Sequence And Free Sources

This guide is the reading-heavy roadmap for the `2L` portion of the curriculum.

The point of `2L` is not just more doctrine. It is learning how to read denser systems of law that rely on codes, rules, agencies, entity statutes, and carefully layered doctrine.

The working rule is:

1. start with relevant free public material from a current `top-10` law school
2. spend real time in the actual code, rule set, or regulation
3. use commentary only to sharpen structure, not replace reading

## Default 2L Order

1. Evidence
2. Criminal Procedure
3. Business Associations
4. Commercial Law, UCC, and Secured Transactions
5. Administrative Law
6. Professional Responsibility

## Evidence

### Read In This Order

1. Official Federal Rules of Evidence through the U.S. Courts rules portal: <https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/current-rules-practice-procedure>
2. Harvard-hosted H2O Federal Rules of Evidence: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/230-federal-rules-of-evidence/as-printable-html/>
3. CourtListener for any full opinion you want to read without casebook trimming: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>
4. Duke Court Rules guide if you want a stronger procedural source map: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/court-rules>

### First-Pass Priorities

- relevance
- Rule 403
- character evidence basics
- impeachment
- hearsay
- core `803` and `804` exceptions

### Read Slowly

- `FRE 401`
- `FRE 403`
- `FRE 404`
- `FRE 801`
- `FRE 803`
- `FRE 804`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can analyze whether evidence is relevant, whether some exclusionary doctrine blocks it, whether a statement is hearsay, and whether an exception saves it.

## Criminal Procedure

### Read In This Order

1. H2O Criminal Procedure: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/8116-criminal-procedure/>
2. Congress.gov Constitution Annotated: <https://constitution.congress.gov/>
3. Oyez for major Supreme Court cases: <https://www.oyez.org/>
4. Stanford Supreme Court Materials if you want briefs, records, and transcripts around major cases: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/supremecourt>
5. CourtListener for full opinions: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- what counts as a search or seizure
- warrants
- warrant exceptions
- exclusionary rule
- Miranda
- right to counsel

### Read Slowly

- `Katz`
- `Terry`
- `Mapp`
- `Miranda`
- `Gideon`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain how the Fourth, Fifth, and Sixth Amendments constrain investigation, interrogation, and prosecution.

## Business Associations

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard-hosted H2O Business Associations: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/15328-business-associations/>
2. Duke Business Associations guide: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/business-associations/>
3. Stanford Transactional Law Research if you want a stronger map of filings, company materials, and deal research: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/transactionallaw>
4. Delaware Code Online, Title 8: <https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/>
5. Delaware Division of Corporations law page: <https://corp.delaware.gov/DElaw/>
6. Columbia CLS Blue Sky Blog for current governance and securities developments: <https://clsbluesky.law.columbia.edu/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- agency
- actual and apparent authority
- partnerships
- LLCs
- corporations
- fiduciary duties
- business judgment rule

### Read Slowly

- the agency chapters
- the corporation-formation chapters
- the fiduciary-duty chapters
- key Delaware statutory sections governing formation and board authority

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain how entity choice, authority, fiduciary obligation, and Delaware corporate structure fit together.

## Commercial Law, UCC, And Secured Transactions

### Read In This Order

1. Duke UCC research guide: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/ucc/>
2. Harvard statutes guide: <https://guides.library.harvard.edu/law/statutes>
3. Penn statutory research guide: <https://law.upenn.libguides.com/researchfundamentals/statutes>
4. Stanford Transactional Law Research for deal documents, company materials, and practical research context: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/transactionallaw>
5. Uniform Law Commission UCC page: <https://www.uniformlaws.org/ucc>
6. Uniform Law Commission current UCC acts and amendments: <https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/catalog/current/ucc>
7. Your jurisdiction's enacted UCC text

### First-Pass Priorities

- Article 2 scope
- merchants
- battle of the forms
- warranties
- attachment
- perfection
- priority basics

### Read Slowly

- `UCC 2-102`
- `UCC 2-104`
- `UCC 2-207`
- `UCC 2-313` to `2-315`
- Article 9 definitions
- Article 9 attachment and perfection provisions

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can move through a UCC problem by checking scope, definitions, operative rule, and then priority structure in that order.

## Administrative Law

### Read In This Order

1. Harvard-hosted H2O Administrative Law: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/8962-administrative-law/>
2. Penn The Regulatory Review: <https://www.theregreview.org/>
3. Duke Federal Administrative Law guide: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/federal-administrative-law/>
4. Stanford Federal Legislative History Research if you need a stronger bridge from enacted statute to legislative record: <https://guides.law.stanford.edu/fedleghistory>
5. Federal Register: <https://www.federalregister.gov/>
6. eCFR: <https://www.ecfr.gov/>
7. GovInfo: <https://www.govinfo.gov/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- agency structure
- delegation
- rulemaking
- adjudication
- judicial review
- standards of review

### Read Slowly

- the APA sections the casebook assigns
- one proposed rule in the Federal Register
- the codified version of that rule in the eCFR

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can explain how a statute becomes agency action and how that action becomes reviewable law.

## Professional Responsibility

### Read In This Order

1. ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct: <https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/?lang=en>
2. ABA table of contents: <https://www.americanbar.org/content/aba-cms-dotorg/en/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/model_rules_of_professional_conduct_table_of_contents/>
3. Harvard-hosted H2O Professional Responsibility: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/15389-professional-responsibility/>
4. Duke Legal Ethics guide: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/legal-ethics/>

### First-Pass Priorities

- competence
- confidentiality
- privilege
- current-client and former-client conflicts
- candor to the tribunal
- truthfulness to others

### Read Slowly

- `Rule 1.1`
- `Rule 1.6`
- `Rule 1.7`
- `Rule 1.9`
- `Rule 3.3`
- `Rule 4.1`

### Good Stopping Point For A First Serious Pass

You can identify which professional-conduct rule governs before reducing the problem to instinct or vague fairness.

## Common 2L Expansion Paths

If you finish the core `2L` reading and want more, the traditional next subjects are:

- remedies
- family law
- trusts and estates
- federal courts
- tax
- bankruptcy
- legislation and regulation

Apply the same resource rule there:

- top-10-school public materials first
- official statutory or regulatory text second
- commercial supplements only if the free pass still leaves the area muddy
