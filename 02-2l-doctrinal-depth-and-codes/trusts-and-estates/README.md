# Trusts And Estates

## Why It Is Here

Wills, trusts, and estates is a `legacy MEE` bar subject — often two MEE topics, `Decedents' Estates` and `Trusts and Future Interests` — that the `NextGen UBE` drops from foundational testing. It sits outside the `NextGen` core; build it for a legacy or transition jurisdiction, or for estate-planning practice. Confirm your jurisdiction first.

It is a `code-and-common-law` subject: read it the way you read the UCC, anchored on the `Uniform Probate Code` and `Uniform Trust Code`, with state variations on top.

## Master These First

- intestate succession and the share structure
- will execution: formalities, revocation, and the harmless-error / substantial-compliance trend
- will components: integration, incorporation by reference, ademption, and lapse
- trust creation: the required elements, settlor intent, and the res
- types of trusts: express, resulting, constructive, and charitable
- the powers, duties, and fiduciary standard of a trustee
- future interests and the `Rule Against Perpetuities` — shared with property

## Common Exam Traps

- start in the `UPC`/`UTC` default, then apply the state variation — do not reason from a generic "common law" alone
- keep `revocation by act` distinct from `revocation by subsequent instrument`, and watch dependent relative revocation
- distinguish `ademption` (specific gifts), `abatement` (order of payment), and `lapse` (predeceasing beneficiaries and anti-lapse statutes)
- a trust needs a `res`, intent, and ascertainable beneficiaries — a missing element may leave a resulting trust
- run `RAP` on contingent future interests; class gifts and powers of appointment are the classic traps
