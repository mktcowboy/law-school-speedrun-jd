# Week 3: Criminal Law Basics

## Focus

Learn the architecture of crime: prohibited act, culpable mental state, causation, and defenses.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- `actus reus`
- `mens rea`
- causation in criminal law
- homicide ladder basics
- inchoate offenses at a high level
- core defenses including self-defense, insanity, and intoxication

## Primary Reading

- Harvard H2O Criminal Law Spring 2021: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/2459-criminal-law-spring-2021/>
- Pull the underlying statute whenever a case turns on wording
- CourtListener for full opinions: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

Cases and concepts to know:

- `Martin v. State`
- `Regina v. Cunningham`
- `People v. Conley`
- `State v. Norman`

## Reading Method

For each offense, ask:

- what act is required
- what mental state is required
- what result is required
- what defenses negate liability

## What To Build

- one matrix for `purpose`, `knowledge`, `recklessness`, `negligence`
- one homicide ladder
- one defenses chart
- one distinction sheet for `common law` versus `MPC-style` framing

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: actus reus and omissions
- Day 2: mens rea and mistake
- Day 3: causation and homicide
- Day 4: attempt, conspiracy, accomplice liability
- Day 5: justification defenses
- Day 6: excuse defenses and synthesis writing

## Writing Assignment

- Analyze a homicide hypo with disputed mental state.
- Analyze a self-defense hypo with an imperfect factual record.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- `Examples & Explanations for Criminal Law`

## Week Exit Standard

You can explain why criminal liability is never just about bad outcomes. It is about a required act, a required mental state, and the fit between the two.
