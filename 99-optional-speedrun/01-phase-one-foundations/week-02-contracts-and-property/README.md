# Week 2: Contracts And Property

## Focus

Learn exchange and ownership.

Contracts teaches how promises become enforceable. Property teaches how legal systems allocate control over things, land, and access.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- offer and acceptance
- consideration
- promissory estoppel
- expectation, reliance, and restitution remedies
- possession
- the bundle of rights
- estates in land
- landlord-tenant basics
- servitudes at a high level

## Primary Reading

### Contracts

- Harvard H2O Contracts casebook: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/11568-contracts/>
- Re-read the Restatement and statutory excerpts embedded in the casebook
- Save deeper UCC work for the later commercial-law pass

Cases and concepts to know:

- `Hamer v. Sidway`
- `Lucy v. Zehmer`
- `Hawkins v. McGee`
- objective theory of assent

### Property

- Harvard H2O Open Source Property: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/510-open-source-property/>
- CourtListener for full opinions: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

Cases and concepts to know:

- `Pierson v. Post`
- `Johnson v. M'Intosh`
- `Jacque v. Steenberg Homes`

## What To Build

- one contract formation flowchart
- one remedies chart with `expectation`, `reliance`, and `restitution`
- one property map covering `possession`, `right to exclude`, `estates`, and `landlord-tenant`
- one glossary of property vocabulary

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: offer, acceptance, objective assent
- Day 2: consideration and promissory estoppel
- Day 3: remedies and defenses overview
- Day 4: possession and acquisition
- Day 5: estates and future interests at a basic level
- Day 6: landlord-tenant, servitudes, synthesis writing

## Writing Assignment

- Write a short contract analysis on whether a promise is enforceable and what remedy fits.
- Write a short property analysis on who has the better entitlement and why.
