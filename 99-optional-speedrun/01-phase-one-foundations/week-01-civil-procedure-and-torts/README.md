# Week 1: Civil Procedure And Torts

## Focus

Learn how a lawsuit moves and why civil wrongs create liability.

This is the best first week because it teaches:

- what courts need before they can act
- how parties frame claims and defenses
- how lawyers prove negligence and other tort theories

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- subject-matter jurisdiction
- personal jurisdiction at a high level
- notice pleading under `Rule 8`
- motions under `Rule 12`
- discovery basics under `Rule 26`
- summary judgment under `Rule 56`
- intentional torts
- negligence and duty/breach/causation/damages

## Primary Reading

- Harvard H2O Civil Procedure 2024: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/9188-civil-procedure-2024/>
- Official U.S. Courts FRCP page: <https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/current-rules-practice-procedure/federal-rules-civil-procedure>
- CourtListener for full opinions: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

Read closely:

- `FRCP 1`
- `FRCP 4`
- `FRCP 8`
- `FRCP 12`
- `FRCP 26`
- `FRCP 56`

Landmark cases to know:

- `International Shoe`
- `Twombly`
- `Iqbal`

### Torts

- Harvard H2O Torts!: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/2566-torts/>
- CourtListener for full opinions: <https://www.courtlistener.com/>

Landmark cases to know:

- `Vosburg v. Putney`
- `Garratt v. Dailey`
- `Palsgraf`
- `United States v. Carroll Towing Co.`

## What To Build

- one chart comparing `subject-matter jurisdiction` and `personal jurisdiction`
- one chart for `intentional torts` versus `negligence`
- one attack outline for the path from `complaint` to `summary judgment`
- one negligence checklist with each element and common defenses

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: court structure, jurisdiction, FRCP 1 and 4
- Day 2: pleading, motions to dismiss, Twombly and Iqbal
- Day 3: discovery and summary judgment
- Day 4: intentional torts and defenses
- Day 5: negligence structure
- Day 6: causation, damages, defenses, synthesis writing

## Writing Assignment

Write two short answers:

- A plaintiff files in federal court. Explain the court-power and pleading questions before the case can move.
- A negligence hypo involving duty, breach, actual cause, proximate cause, and comparative fault.

## Week Exit Standard

You can say, out loud and without notes:

- the difference between a court's power over a case and the merits of a claim
- the elements of negligence
- why `Palsgraf` still matters
