# Week 4: Legal Research And Synthesis

## Focus

Learn how to move between cases, statutes, rules, and secondary sources without getting lost.

This week is where doctrinal reading becomes legal workflow.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to:

- find and read primary law
- use secondary sources to orient quickly
- brief a statute separately from a case
- trace a doctrine across rules, opinions, and commentary
- produce a short research memo-style answer

## Drills

### Research drill 1

Find:

- one FRCP provision
- one Federal Rule of Evidence provision
- one constitutional case
- one criminal law doctrine entry in your casebook or notes

### Research drill 2

Take one topic from Weeks 1 to 3 and gather:

- one case
- one statute or rule
- one secondary explanation
- one short written synthesis

## What To Build

- one personal `research map` listing your go-to sources
- one `citation cheat sheet`
- three completed templates from the `templates` folder

## Writing Assignment

Write a `500 to 800 word` mini memo answering a question from Weeks 1 to 3 using at least:

- one case
- one rule or statute
- one secondary source

## If You Buy One Supplement

- no subject supplement needed here
- if you want a citation book, buy `The Bluebook` only if you truly need formal citation depth

## Week Exit Standard

You can answer: where do I start, what source controls, and how do I prove it?
