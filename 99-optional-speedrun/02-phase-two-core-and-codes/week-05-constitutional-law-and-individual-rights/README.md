# Week 5: Constitutional Law And Individual Rights

## Focus

Shift into constitutional structure and rights analysis, with emphasis on speech, religion, equal protection, and due process.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- state action
- judicial review as background architecture
- levels of scrutiny at a working level
- First Amendment speech basics
- Free Exercise and Establishment basics
- Equal Protection analysis
- Procedural and substantive Due Process basics

## Primary Reading

- National Archives Constitution transcript: <https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution>
- Yale Avalon constitutional document record: <https://avalon.law.yale.edu/subject_menus/constpap.asp>
- Congress.gov Constitution Annotated: <https://constitution.congress.gov/>
- Columbia Global Freedom of Expression: <https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/>
- Oyez constitutional cases: <https://www.oyez.org/>

Cases to know:

- `Marbury v. Madison`
- `United States v. Lopez`
- `Brown v. Board of Education`
- `Griswold v. Connecticut` through `Obergefell v. Hodges` and `Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization`
- `New York Times v. Sullivan`
- `Tinker v. Des Moines`
- `Employment Division v. Smith`

## What To Build

- one `levels of scrutiny` chart
- one speech-analysis checklist
- one equal-protection checklist
- one due-process checklist

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: structure, judicial review, state action
- Day 2: speech doctrine
- Day 3: religion clauses
- Day 4: equal protection
- Day 5: due process
- Day 6: mixed constitutional writing

## Writing Assignment

- Write one short First Amendment issue spotter.
- Write one equal protection or due process answer using the correct level of scrutiny framework.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- a concise constitutional law supplement is helpful, but open-source materials are enough for the first pass

## Week Exit Standard

You can identify the right constitutional framework before talking about who should win.
