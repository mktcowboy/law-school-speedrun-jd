# Week 7: Business Associations And Corporations

## Focus

Learn the legal forms through which people organize business activity and the fiduciary duties that govern internal decision-making.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- sole proprietorships, partnerships, LLCs, and corporations
- agency basics
- authority
- fiduciary duties
- derivative versus direct claims at a high level
- the business judgment rule

## Primary Reading

- Harvard-hosted H2O Business Associations: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/15328-business-associations/>
- Delaware Code Online, Title 8: <https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/>
- Delaware Division of Corporations law page: <https://corp.delaware.gov/DElaw/>
- Columbia CLS Blue Sky Blog: <https://clsbluesky.law.columbia.edu/>

Cases and concepts to know:

- `Walkovszky v. Carlton`
- `Meinhard v. Salmon`
- `Smith v. Van Gorkom`

## What To Build

- one entity-choice chart
- one authority chart for `actual`, `apparent`, and `inherent` style questions
- one fiduciary-duty checklist
- one business-judgment-rule outline

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: agency and authority
- Day 2: partnerships and LLCs
- Day 3: corporation structure and roles
- Day 4: fiduciary duties
- Day 5: shareholder litigation and governance
- Day 6: synthesis writing

## Writing Assignment

- Compare which entity form best fits three different business scenarios.
- Analyze one fiduciary-duty problem involving loyalty or care.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- buy only if the governance vocabulary is slowing you down

## Week Exit Standard

You can explain not just how businesses are formed, but how legal duty travels inside them.
