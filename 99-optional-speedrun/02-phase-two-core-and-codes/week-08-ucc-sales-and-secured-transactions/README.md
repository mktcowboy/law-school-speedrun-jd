# Week 8: UCC Sales And Secured Transactions

## Focus

Move from common-law contracts into code-based commercial law.

This week matters because it changes your reading posture: you now have to extract rules directly from statutory text and defined terms.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- when `Article 2` applies
- formation and battle-of-the-forms basics
- warranties at a working level
- risk of loss at a high level
- attachment
- perfection
- priority basics

## Primary Reading

- Duke UCC research guide: <https://law.duke.edu/lib/research-guides/ucc/>
- Uniform Law Commission UCC page: <https://www.uniformlaws.org/ucc>
- Uniform Law Commission current UCC acts and amendments: <https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/catalog/current/ucc>
- Your jurisdiction's enacted UCC text

Start with:

- `UCC 2-102`
- `UCC 2-104`
- `UCC 2-207`
- `UCC 2-313` to `2-315`
- Article 9 overview and definitions

## What To Build

- one `Article 2` scope chart
- one battle-of-the-forms checklist
- one warranties chart
- one secured-transactions ladder for `attachment -> perfection -> priority`

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: how to read the UCC, scope, merchants
- Day 2: formation and battle of the forms
- Day 3: warranties and remedies
- Day 4: attachment and collateral
- Day 5: perfection and priority
- Day 6: mixed code-reading and writing

## Writing Assignment

- Write one UCC sales issue spotter.
- Write one secured-transactions priority issue spotter.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- wait until after the free pass
- this is exactly the kind of subject where a supplement helps only after you have wrestled with the code itself

## Week Exit Standard

You can move through a statute in order and explain how definitions, scope provisions, and priority rules interact.
