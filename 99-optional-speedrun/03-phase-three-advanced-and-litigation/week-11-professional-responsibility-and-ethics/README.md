# Week 11: Professional Responsibility And Ethics

## Focus

Study the duties that govern lawyers themselves.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- duty of competence
- confidentiality and privilege
- conflicts of interest
- candor to the tribunal
- duties to clients, courts, and third parties
- basic advertising and solicitation limits

## Primary Reading

- ABA Model Rules portal: <https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/?lang=en>
- ABA table of contents: <https://www.americanbar.org/content/aba-cms-dotorg/en/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/model_rules_of_professional_conduct_table_of_contents/>
- Harvard-hosted H2O Professional Responsibility: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/15389-professional-responsibility/>

Read especially:

- `Rule 1.1`
- `Rule 1.6`
- `Rule 1.7`
- `Rule 1.9`
- `Rule 3.3`
- `Rule 4.1`

## What To Build

- one confidentiality versus privilege chart
- one current-client and former-client conflicts chart
- one candor and truthfulness checklist
- one ethics issue-spotting outline

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: competence, scope, communication, fees
- Day 2: confidentiality and privilege
- Day 3: conflicts
- Day 4: litigation duties and candor
- Day 5: duties to third parties and business development
- Day 6: ethics writing

## Writing Assignment

- Analyze one conflict problem.
- Analyze one confidentiality/candor problem where duties collide.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- not necessary on the first pass if you read the rules directly and outline them carefully

## Week Exit Standard

You can identify the rule that governs the lawyer before drifting into gut-feel morality.
