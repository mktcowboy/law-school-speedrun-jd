# Week 12: Capstone Synthesis And Bar Bridge

## Focus

Pull the subjects together and convert doctrinal understanding into exam-usable structure.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should have:

- one cleaned-up outline for every subject in this curriculum
- one attack outline for every subject
- several mixed-subject issue spotters completed
- a clear next step into either `NextGen UBE` or legacy-jurisdiction prep

## Capstone Tasks

### 1. Cross-subject synthesis

Write mixed hypos that force interaction between:

- torts and evidence
- contracts and UCC
- constitutional law and criminal procedure
- professional responsibility and civil procedure

### 2. Outline compression

Take each subject from:

- full outline
- to attack outline
- to one-page checklist

### 3. Rule recall

Test yourself closed-book on:

- negligence
- personal jurisdiction
- offer/acceptance/consideration
- mens rea
- equal protection
- Miranda
- attachment/perfection/priority
- hearsay
- conflicts

As of `June 2, 2026`:

- `July 2026` is the opening administration window for the `NextGen UBE` in participating jurisdictions.
- Some states are still in transition, so verify your state before choosing practice format.

## What To Do Next

### If you want doctrinal depth

- stretch each subject into a month
- add more writing
- buy targeted supplements only where your understanding is thin

### If you want bar alignment

- pick your target jurisdiction
- confirm whether it is `NextGen` or legacy
- start using official NCBE framing
- add timed writing and multiple-choice work that matches that format

## Final Deliverable

Write a `2 to 4 page` reflection answering:

- which subjects feel intuitive now
- which still require mechanical drilling
- what your next `8 to 12 weeks` should prioritize
