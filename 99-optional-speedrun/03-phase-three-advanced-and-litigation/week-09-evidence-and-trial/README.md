# Week 9: Evidence And Trial

## Focus

Learn what information can come into court and why.

## Outcomes

By the end of the week, you should be able to explain:

- relevance
- Rule 403 balancing
- character evidence basics
- hearsay and the logic of hearsay
- common exclusions and exceptions
- impeachment at a working level

## Primary Reading

- H2O Evidence materials: <https://opencasebook.org/featured/>
- Official Federal Rules of Evidence through the U.S. Courts rules portal: <https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/current-rules-practice-procedure>
- Harvard-hosted H2O Federal Rules of Evidence: <https://opencasebook.org/casebooks/230-federal-rules-of-evidence/as-printable-html/>

Read closely:

- `FRE 401`
- `FRE 402`
- `FRE 403`
- `FRE 404`
- `FRE 801`
- `FRE 802`
- `FRE 803`
- `FRE 804`

## What To Build

- one admissibility checklist
- one hearsay flowchart
- one chart for `404`, impeachment, and habit versus character
- one mini trial notebook with objections and responses

## Suggested Daily Split

- Day 1: relevance and 403
- Day 2: character, conduct, impeachment
- Day 3: hearsay definition and non-hearsay
- Day 4: exclusions and exceptions
- Day 5: confrontation and trial application
- Day 6: objection drills and writing

## Writing Assignment

- Take a short witness narrative and list all admissibility fights.
- Write one hearsay analysis in full sentences, not just labels.

## If You Buy One Supplement

- evidence is a good subject for a concise supplement if hearsay is slowing you down badly

## Week Exit Standard

You can answer not only whether a fact matters, but whether the jury is allowed to hear it.
