# Pre-1L Methods And Mindset

Most self-study plans fail not because the reader picks the wrong subjects, but because they start reading cases before they know how to read a case.

This folder is the methods bridge. It teaches the mechanical skills every `T10/Ivy` `1L` is expected to absorb in the first weeks: how to read an opinion, how to brief it, how to turn a stack of cases into a rule, how to build an outline, and how to write a short `IRAC` answer.

Work through it once before [01-1l-foundation-year](../01-1l-foundation-year/README.md). Come back whenever your reading starts to feel like passive highlighting.

## Why This Exists

Top law schools front-load method. Harvard's `Zero-L` pre-orientation course, `1L` legal-methods weeks, and the first month of every doctrinal class exist to install the same habits before doctrine arrives.

A self-study reader has no cold call, no professor, and no graded memo to force those habits. This folder is the substitute.

## The Mindset

- you are training a `skill`, not memorizing a `subject`
- the goal of `1L` reading is fluency in legal reasoning, not coverage
- every case you read should leave behind one `rule` you can state in a sentence
- understanding comes before memorization, always
